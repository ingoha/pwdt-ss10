Integrator - Level II Training

Overview

Goals

The major goal of the Integrator Level II Training is to help the participants in becoming familiar with the scripting extensions of the Integrator (formerly Cloverleaf) message broker. Knowledge of the scripting extensions is required in order to take full advantage of the message processing capabilities of the Integrator.

The goal of this training is to provide the participants with the necessary practical skills for using the software product to its greatest potential, without having to "reinvent the weel". This training is based on experiences gained by the Integrator implementators employed by Quovadx (formerly Healthcare.com), its subsidiaries or Integrator distributors. Their exposure to a variety of problems and their methodologies and procedures developed working at a multitude of customers sites has determined the content of this training. By taking their experiences as a starting point this training is able to focus on a number of issues one is likely to encounter when creating connections using Integrator.

This training builds upon the Level I training class and extends the knowledge gained during that training. Whereas the Level I training focuses on use of the GUI, the Level II training focuses on the usage of the available Tcl script extensions.

The Level II Training consists of a number of modules that each cover a particular topic. Each module contains multiple examples. The training material contains a number of optional modules that will be covered, depending on the overall pace of the training or at the request of the participants.

After having completed the Level II Training the participant will be able to change the content of messages and/or the routing of messages using the Tcl-based scripting extensions as well as the GUIs.

Overview

Standard Level II training

This training covers the following topics:
Engine architecture A review of the engine architecture introduced during the Level I class.
Tcl language basics An introduction of standard Tcl and Extended Tcl commands (e.g. if-then, switch-case, keyed lists, string parsing, file I/O) and the Integrator specific extensions to Tcl. This includes the message extensions and the Integrator extensions to Tcl dealing with the parsing and encoding of messages (general record managers, a.k.a. a parser/encoder).
Java language basics An introduction of the Integrator specific classes. This includes the message extensions classes dealing with the parsing and encoding of messages (general record managers, a.k.a. a parser/encoder). The trainee is expected to be familiar with Java programming.
User Points of Control The various UPoCs will be introduced as well as and their usage. This includes a discussion of the various procedure types (Code fragments, TPS, TrxId determination).
Programmable Driver Language The PDL (Programmable Driver Language) will be discussed in generic terms and a couple of examples will be discussed. The PDL language is used to create drivers for use with transport protocols (at the Session level) that are not supported by one of the standard protocols listed in the Network Configurator.

ODBC related modules (optional)

ODBC Architecture The ODBC architecture and its various layers.
SQL statements How to execute SQL statements and fetch the result of a statement
Usage in the Integrator A discussion of the usage of the ODBC extensions in the various UPoCs.
